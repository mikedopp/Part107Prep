# Product

## Register

product

## Users

People preparing for the FAA Part 107 Remote Pilot knowledge test who learn best by working through realistic local flight decisions instead of memorizing isolated definitions. They may use the trainer at a desk or on a phone and need chart, weather, regulation, and risk information translated into a confident preflight sequence.

## Product Purpose

Teach Part 107 judgment through practice exams, focused drills, visual chart lessons, and place-based mission scenarios. Success means a learner can look at a sectional chart and METAR, identify what matters, decide whether a flight is legal and sensible, and recognize how the FAA will test that reasoning.

## Brand Personality

Relaxed, confident, experienced. The voice is a drone pilot who has seen avoidable mistakes, explains the signal without drama, and gives a clear go, modify, or no-go call.

## Anti-references

Do not resemble a dense government manual, a generic card dashboard, a gamified children's quiz, or an aviation display that assumes the learner already understands every abbreviation. Avoid legal mush, ornamental cockpit styling, unexplained acronyms, and walls of equally weighted facts.

## Design Principles

1. Make the decision sequence obvious: launch site, airspace, weather, hazards, verdict.
2. Point to the exact chart evidence before explaining the rule.
3. Decode weather into operational consequences, not just expanded abbreviations.
4. Separate legal permission from good pilot judgment.
5. End each scenario with the exam traps the learner should now recognize.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA. All scenario, chart, quiz, and modal interactions must work with keyboard, touch, and assistive technology. Never rely on color alone, respect reduced motion, use meaningful figure descriptions, and keep critical copy readable on narrow screens.
