# Part107Prep v1.5 FAA map sources

## Source contract

All four production assets are direct crops of FAA Sectional Aeronautical Raster Chart GeoTIFFs. No generative image tools, labels, arrows, markers, or UI callouts were applied to the chart pixels. Scenario explanations and numbered evidence markers should remain semantic HTML overlays.

- FAA VFR raster chart catalog: <https://www.faa.gov/air_traffic/flight_info/aeronav/digital_products/vfr/>
- Edition used: effective July 9, 2026
- Next listed edition: September 3, 2026
- Source format: FAA 300 dpi, 8-bit GeoTIFF with FAA world file and NAD83 Lambert Conformal Conic metadata
- Output format: indexed PNG, 192 colors, lossless PNG compression

These crops are training references, not an operational briefing. Before flight, check current FAA airspace authorization, NOTAMs, TFRs, weather, local launch restrictions, and land-manager rules. Sectional charts do not depict LAANC grid values or every local restriction.

## Hotspot coordinate format

Suggested rectangles use percentages of the final PNG in this order:

`x, y, width, height`

The origin is the upper-left corner. Values are intentionally a little larger than the printed symbol or label so a keyboard/touch focus ring does not hide the evidence.

Crop notes: Old Faithful is close to the north neatline of the Salt Lake City Sectional. West Yellowstone Airport (WYS), the nearest public-airport reference most learners will expect, is north of this chart edge and is not present in the crop. Stanford Field (U12) is the nearest readable airport data block inside this crop, but it is not the nearest airport to Old Faithful. Do not label it as such. The view preserves the chart-edge fact because recognizing missing briefing data is part of good judgment.

| Evidence cue | Suggested rectangle | Teaching use |
|---|---:|---|
| Launch point, Old Faithful | `62.5, 13.5, 11.5, 8.5` | Tie the coordinate to the printed Old Faithful label, then explain that chart visibility does not grant land-manager permission. |
| Visible airport context, Stanford Field (U12) | `0.0, 85.0, 27.0, 11.5` | Decode the visible airport block while clearly stating that WYS is off-chart and must be checked separately. |
| Class E magenta vignette west of the park | `19.5, 0.0, 25.0, 44.0` | Explain the 700-foot Class E transition cue without confusing it with the park boundary. |
| Terrain / maximum elevation figure `107` | `67.0, 37.0, 10.0, 11.0` | Read `107` as 10,700 feet MSL and compare it with Mount Sheridan at 10,305 feet. |
| Yellowstone National Park label and boundary cue | `89.0, 23.0, 11.0, 12.0` | Use the chart as the location cue, then teach that the National Park Service launch and landing restriction comes from land-manager rules, not the sectional itself. |

Coverage uncertainty: The exact Old Faithful coordinate is positively inside this chart and crop. The crop cannot show the nearest public airport because the scenario sits at a sectional edge. This is intentional and should be disclosed in the lesson.
